# Fatty Liver Prevention: Daily Habits for a Healthy Liver

Excess fat in the liver develops from poor eating habits, sedentary behaviour and unhealthy lifestyle choices — but it can be prevented through manageable daily routines.

## Diet, Exercise & Alcohol

Eat vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins and fruit, and avoid excess fried foods, processed snacks and sugary drinks — beneficial fats from nuts, seeds and olive oil support liver function, and adequate water aids toxin removal. Moderate activity like yoga, swimming, jogging or a 30-minute walk most days reduces liver fat, and limiting alcohol lowers fatty-liver risk.

## Liver-Friendly Foods & Stress

Garlic, turmeric, ginger, berries and leafy greens actively support liver function and reduce fat accumulation. While stress doesn't directly cause fatty liver, it encourages poor eating, inadequate sleep and weight gain — so deep breathing, meditation, gentle yoga and nature walks help manage it.

## Medicines, Natural Support & Sleep

Certain medications burden the liver when overused — follow medical guidance and avoid excessive non-prescription drugs. High-fibre foods, adequate water and liver-supporting vegetables like leafy greens, lemon and beetroot sustain liver strength. Quality sleep enables hepatic self-repair; insufficient sleep slows metabolism and increases liver fat.

## Live a Healthy Lifestyle

Avoiding smoking and environmental toxins protects the liver, and consistent routines with regular meals, exercise and hydration maintain liver health over the long term.
